Resume Guide

A resume is your personal marketing tool. It’s a summary of your skills, education, and experience designed to showcase why you are the perfect fit for a job.

Personal Info

Include your name, professional email, phone number, and a link to your LinkedIn profile.

Education

List your degree, university name, and graduation year. Include relevant coursework.

Skills

Highlight technical skills and soft skills that match the job description.

Experience

Use bullet points to describe your past roles, internships, or relevant projects.

Do's

  • Tailor it to the job description
  • Use a clean, professional format
  • Highlight achievements, not just duties

Don'ts

  • Don't include a personal photo (unless requested)
  • Don't lie about your skills or experience
  • Don't make it longer than 1-2 pages

Quick Resume Tips

1

Keep it short

Recruiters spend just seconds scanning a resume. Keep it to one page if you're a student or recent graduate.

2

Use bullet points

Avoid long paragraphs. Use clear, concise bullet points to make your experience easy to read.

3

Avoid spelling mistakes

Proofread multiple times. A single typo can make you look careless and unprofessional.

4

Use action words

Start bullet points with strong verbs like "Achieved", "Developed", and "Managed".

Interview Preparation

Acing an interview requires more than just knowing your resume. It's about presentation and confidence.

Types of Interviews

Be prepared for behavioral, technical, panel, and phone/video interviews. Each requires a slightly different approach.

Body Language Tips

Maintain eye contact, sit up straight, offer a firm handshake, and remember to smile. Non-verbal cues matter.

Confidence Tips

Practice common questions out loud, research the company thoroughly, and take deep breaths to calm your nerves.

Important Interview Questions

Common questions you are almost guaranteed to face.

Sample Answer: Keep it professional. Start with your current situation (e.g., degree), transition into relevant past experiences, and finish with why you're excited about this specific opportunity.

Sample Answer: Review the job description and align your top 2-3 skills with their needs. "You should hire me because my background in X and my ability to do Y will help your team achieve Z."

Sample Answer: Mention 2-3 strengths with examples. Pick strengths relevant to the job, such as problem-solving, teamwork, or attention to detail, and back them up with real situations.

Sample Answer: Mention one genuine weakness but quickly explain the steps you are actively taking to improve upon it. This shows self-awareness and a growth mindset.

Sample Answer: Show ambition and ensure your goals align with the role. "In 5 years, I hope to have deepened my expertise in [field] and taken on more leadership responsibilities within this company."

Skill Development

Focus on these core competencies to stand out in the modern workplace.

Communication

Active listening, clear writing, and articulate speaking are universally required across all roles.

Time Management

Prioritizing tasks, meeting deadlines, and efficiently managing your workload shows reliability.

Basic Digital Skills

Proficiency in common software (Word, Excel), email etiquette, and basic troubleshooting.
